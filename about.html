About LACSEF

The Los Angeles County Science & Engineering Fair has been inspiring young scientists and engineers since 1950. Each year we bring together hundreds of students from across LA County to present original research, compete for scholarships, and connect with the broader scientific community.

Our Mission

LACSEF exists to cultivate scientific curiosity and critical thinking in K–12 students throughout Los Angeles County. We provide a supportive, competitive environment where students can pursue original research under the guidance of qualified mentors, present their findings to expert judges, and gain recognition for their achievements in STEM.

Our History

Founded in 1950 by science teachers from LAUSD, Pasadena Unified, and the Archdiocese of Los Angeles, LACSEF is one of the longest-running regional science fairs in the United States. What began with roughly 150 student projects has grown to over 1,200 projects spanning 38 categories. The fair is entirely volunteer-run — every board and committee member serves without compensation — and its top finalists compete at the Regeneron International Science and Engineering Fair (ISEF) against students from 80+ countries.

Our Story

1950

Science teachers from LAUSD, Pasadena Unified, and the Archdiocese of Los Angeles launch the first fair at the California Museum of Science and Industry, with about 150 student projects — boosted in spirit by post-Sputnik enthusiasm for science education.

1970s

LACSEF incorporates as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit and establishes a formal Science Fair Advisory Committee, cementing its organizational structure.

1989

The Whittier Earthquake forces a venue change; the fair moves to the Los Angeles Sports Arena for the next 15 years. Submissions grow steadily, approaching 800 projects by the late 1990s.

2000

50th Anniversary fair moves to the Los Angeles Convention Center with 850+ projects and nearly 40 business and industry sponsors. A Mentor Program launches the following year, pairing students with working professionals.

2010

The 60th annual fair moves to the Pasadena Convention Center with ~1,200 projects across 38 categories. LACSEF affiliates with Intel (now Regeneron) ISEF, giving top finalists the chance to compete against students from more than 80 countries.

What We Do

Student Competition

Students in grades 6–12 submit original research projects across 35 scientific categories, judged by working professionals and academics from across Southern California.

Awards & Scholarships

We distribute over $50,000 in awards and scholarships annually, recognizing excellence across categories and connecting outstanding finalists with universities and industry sponsors.

public

Pathway to ISEF

Top LACSEF finalists earn the opportunity to represent Los Angeles County at the Regeneron International Science and Engineering Fair, the world's largest pre-college science competition.
