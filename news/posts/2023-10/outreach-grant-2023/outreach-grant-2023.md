The LA County Science & Engineering Fair has received a **$250,000 grant** from the Herrera Family Foundation to fund science access programs in underserved LA County schools through 2026.

## What the Grant Funds

The three-year grant will support three programs that begin rolling out in January 2024:

### Science Mentorship Kits

Thirty curated experiment kits — covering topics from water quality testing to basic electronics — will be distributed to partner schools in high-need areas. Each kit is designed to support 20–30 students and comes with curriculum aligned to California Next Generation Science Standards.

### Transportation Subsidies

One of the most consistent barriers to fair participation is simply getting to the venue. The grant will cover round-trip transportation from 40 participating schools to both Fair Day and the California State Science Fair, for students who would otherwise be unable to attend.

### Teacher Professional Development

Each partner school will receive two full-day workshops annually — one in October covering project design and safety, one in January covering data analysis and presentation — taught by LACSEF staff and volunteer scientists.

## Why This Investment

> "Science fairs are one of the few places where a student's idea matters more than their zip code. This grant is an attempt to make that a reality, not just a platitude." — Rosa Herrera, Herrera Family Foundation

## Impact Goal

By 2026, LACSEF aims to increase participation from schools in the bottom quartile of per-pupil county spending by 40% compared to 2022 baseline levels. We will publish progress reports annually.
