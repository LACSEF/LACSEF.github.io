We need **200 judges** with STEM backgrounds for Fair Day 2024 — and we are 60 short of that goal. If you work in science, engineering, medicine, mathematics, or technology, please consider giving a morning.

## The Time Commitment

Judging runs from **8:00 AM to 1:00 PM on March 22**, with an optional virtual orientation on February 28. That is five hours, one day, one morning — and students remember it for years.

## Who Qualifies

Any professional, graduate student, or recently retired individual with a degree or equivalent experience in a STEM field. You do not need prior judging experience. You do need:

- Reliable transportation to the Pasadena Convention Center
- The ability to stand and walk for up to four hours
- Patience and a genuine interest in talking with students about their work

## How Judging Works

Each judge is assigned to a single category and evaluates 6–8 projects using a structured rubric. You will score each project independently, then meet briefly with co-judges to calibrate before scores are submitted. The Chief Judge panel handles any ties or borderline cases.

## Why It Matters

> "I came expecting to evaluate students. I left realizing they had evaluated me — my assumptions about what teenagers are capable of, about who counts as a scientist. It reset my baseline." — First-time judge, 2022

## Sign Up

Apply at lacsef.org/judge or email judging@lacsef.org with your name, organization, and STEM field. Applications are accepted through February 15, or until spots are filled.
