Good science requires more than a sound experiment. It requires honest reporting, respect for research subjects, and the integrity to publish results you did not expect. Here is what every LACSEF student must know.

## The Non-Negotiables

**Do not fabricate data.** This means inventing results you did not observe. It is the most serious research misconduct and results in immediate disqualification.

**Do not falsify data.** This means changing results you did observe — removing outliers without justification, adjusting numbers to fit your hypothesis, or selectively reporting trials. This is also fabrication.

**Do not plagiarize.** Copying any passage from any source without quotation and citation is plagiarism, even if you change a few words. Judges check.

**Disclose your sources.** If your project builds on someone else's methodology, instrument, or dataset, say so clearly in your report. Proper citation is not weakness — it shows you understand how science works.

## Human and Animal Subjects

Research involving human participants requires IRB-equivalent approval before you begin. This includes surveys, interviews, and any study where you observe people's behavior.

Vertebrate animal research requires IACUC approval and must comply with the three Rs: Replace, Reduce, Refine. Any vertebrate study without prior approval is disqualified.

## Conflicts of Interest

If your project is funded by, conducted at, or directly benefits a company or organization with a financial stake in the outcome, you must disclose this. Judges are not required to penalize disclosed conflicts, but concealed conflicts will result in disqualification.

## A Note on AI Tools

You may use AI writing assistants for grammar checks, not for generating scientific content. Any section of your report that was substantially written by an AI tool must be disclosed. The science and the reasoning must be yours.
