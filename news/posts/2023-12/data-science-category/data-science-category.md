Starting in 2024, LACSEF will include a dedicated **Data Science & Artificial Intelligence** category in both the Junior and Senior Divisions. This reflects the rapid growth of student projects using computational methods and the recognition that these projects have been difficult to evaluate under existing categories like Mathematics or Computer Science.

## What Qualifies

Projects in this category must:

- Center on an original question answered through data analysis or a trained model
- Include a dataset that the student collected, cleaned, or meaningfully transformed (not just downloaded and passed to a model)
- Demonstrate understanding of model limitations, bias, and uncertainty

Projects that _use_ statistics as a tool but whose primary question is biological or environmental should still enter the relevant domain category.

## What Judges Will Look For

| Criterion                        | Weight |
| -------------------------------- | ------ |
| Clarity of the research question | 20%    |
| Data integrity and provenance    | 25%    |
| Appropriateness of method        | 25%    |
| Interpretation and limitations   | 20%    |
| Presentation                     | 10%    |

## Why This Matters

AI tools are increasingly accessible, which means the bar for novelty is higher. Judges will distinguish between a student who used a pre-built classifier and one who understood why a specific architecture was appropriate for the problem. Depth of understanding matters more than sophistication of tools.

## How to Enter

Select "Data Science & Artificial Intelligence" as your category when registering in the Student Portal. A detailed rubric and example project abstracts from our pilot review committee will be posted in January.
