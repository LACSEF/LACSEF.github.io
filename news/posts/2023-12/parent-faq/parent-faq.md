Every year we answer the same questions from parents and guardians new to the fair. Here are the 15 most common ones.

## Before the Fair

**How much can I help my child?**
You can encourage, proofread for grammar, and help acquire materials. You cannot design the experiment, analyze the data, or write any part of the report. Judges are trained to identify projects where the student cannot explain their own methodology.

**Does it cost money to enter?**
No. Registration is free. Students are responsible for their own materials, but low-cost projects win every year. We also offer a materials grant for students who demonstrate financial need — see the Student Portal.

**My child is in 6th grade. Is this too young?**
Not at all. The Junior Division is designed for grades 6–8. Some of the most creative projects we see come from students encountering research methods for the first time.

**What if my child's school does not have a science fair?**
Students can register independently if a credentialed teacher at any LA County school agrees to sponsor them. The teacher does not need to be at the student's school.

## On Fair Day

**Where is the fair held?**
The 2024 fair will be held at the Pasadena Convention Center, 300 E. Green St., Pasadena, CA 91101.

**Where do we park?**
Parking is available in the Marengo Avenue Garage ($12 flat rate on Fair Day). Arrive by 7:30 AM if you want a spot close to the entrance.

**Can siblings attend?**
Siblings are welcome during public viewing hours: 1:00–3:00 PM on March 22.

**What should my child bring?**
Their display board, lab notebook, any physical materials or samples (if appropriate), and a printed copy of their abstract. Bring water and a snack — students are not dismissed for lunch.

## After the Fair

**When will results be posted?**
Awards are announced at the ceremony beginning at 3:30 PM. The full results list is posted to the website by 6:00 PM the same day.

**What does a state fair bid mean?**
Students who earn a state-fair bid are invited to compete at the California State Science Fair in Sacramento. LACSEF covers registration; travel costs are the family's responsibility. Financial assistance may be available — contact us.

**How do I get my child's board home?**
Students may retrieve boards after the awards ceremony. Boards left after 5:00 PM will be held for one week at the LACSEF office before disposal.
