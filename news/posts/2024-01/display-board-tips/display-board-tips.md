Your display board is often the first thing a judge sees. It should tell your story in under 60 seconds of reading time. We asked three recent grand-prize winners what made the difference.

## The Layout That Works

Most winning boards follow a Z-pattern read: the eye enters at the top-left and sweeps to the bottom-right. Structure yours to match:

1. **Title + Question** (top-center, large)
2. **Background & Hypothesis** (top-left)
3. **Materials & Procedure** (center-left)
4. **Data & Results** (center-right, largest section)
5. **Conclusion & Future Work** (bottom-right)

## What Alumni Told Us

> "I used 11pt font for my data tables. A judge literally couldn't read it from arm's length." — Amir, 2022 Senior Division winner

> "My color palette clashed. The data was great but the board looked chaotic. Stick to two colors plus white." — Jess, 2023 Junior Division winner

> "Leave white space. I tried to fill every inch the first year. The second year I left margins and won." — Daniela, 2023 Engineering grand prize

## Board Specs

- Maximum size: 48" wide × 36" tall (tri-fold)
- No motorized or illuminated elements
- All photos must be printed, not displayed on screens
