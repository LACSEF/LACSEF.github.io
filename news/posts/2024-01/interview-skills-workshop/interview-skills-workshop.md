## Key Takeaways

### Lead with your question, not your background

Judges have read your abstract. Do not spend the first 90 seconds on background. Open with: _"I wanted to know whether X, because Y."_ Then describe what you did.

### The 30-second version matters

Every student should be able to explain their project in 30 seconds without jargon. Practice this until it is automatic — judges use it to calibrate how well you understand your own work.

### Handling "I don't know"

It is not a failure to say you do not know. The right answer is: _"I am not sure — but based on what I found, I would expect..."_ Judges reward intellectual honesty and the ability to reason under uncertainty.

## Practice Questions from the Workshop

1. What would you change about your experimental design?
2. What was the biggest source of error in your data?
3. If you had six more months, what would you test next?
4. Why does your conclusion matter beyond this experiment?
