If you are in 6th, 7th, or 8th grade, the Junior Division is built for you. Here is everything you need to know before Fair Day.

## How It Differs from Senior Division

The Junior Division prioritizes **scientific process over complexity**. Judges want to see that you asked a real question, designed a fair experiment, and drew honest conclusions — not that you had access to a university lab.

Projects are judged on:

- Clarity of the question and hypothesis
- Soundness of experimental design
- Accuracy of data recording
- Quality of analysis and conclusions

## Timeline at a Glance

- **January 15** — Registration opens
- **February 15** — Project abstract due
- **March 1** — Safety forms deadline
- **March 22** — Fair Day (Junior exhibits 8 AM–12 PM)

## On the Day

You will have a 36" × 48" table space. Judges visit in pairs and spend about 8 minutes with each student, including a short Q&A. Bring your lab notebook — judges may ask to see it.

Younger siblings and parents may attend the public viewing from 1:00–3:00 PM.

## First-Timer Tips

- Practice explaining your project out loud to someone who is not a scientist
- Wear comfortable shoes — Fair Day is long
- Bring a snack; lunch is not provided for students
