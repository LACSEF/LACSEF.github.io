The **LACSEF Mentor Match Program** is now accepting student requests for the 2024 fair cycle. Every registered student in grades 9–12 is eligible to request a volunteer mentor.

## How It Works

1. Log in to the Student Portal and select "Request a Mentor"
2. Fill out a short questionnaire about your project topic and what kind of guidance you are looking for
3. We match you with a STEM professional in a related field within five business days
4. Your mentor commits to **two one-hour video check-ins** before Fair Day

## What Mentors Can Help With

- Reviewing your experimental design for logical gaps
- Suggesting relevant prior research to cite
- Coaching you on how to present to judges
- Answering domain-specific questions (not doing the work for you)

> "My mentor was a water treatment engineer. She pointed out a variable in my filtration experiment I had completely missed. That feedback changed everything." — Priya, 2023 participant

## What Mentors Cannot Do

Mentors may not perform lab work, analyze data, write any part of your report, or provide materials. The project must remain entirely the student's own work.

## Deadline

Mentor requests must be submitted by **February 1**. Requests after that date cannot be guaranteed a match before Fair Day.
