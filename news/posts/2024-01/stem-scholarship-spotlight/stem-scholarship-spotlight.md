**Marcus Delgado** entered his first science fair in 7th grade with a project about antibacterial properties of local soil bacteria. Four years later, that curiosity earned him a full-ride scholarship to Caltech.

## The Project That Started It All

Marcus's 2022 Senior Division project — _"Isolation and Characterization of Antibiotic-Producing Actinomycetes from Three Southern California Soil Microbiomes"_ — won the grand prize in the Life Sciences category and a bid to the International Science and Engineering Fair.

"I literally just thought dirt was interesting," he says. "I had no idea it would lead to a career."

## What LACSEF Taught Him

Marcus credits the fair with three things that shaped his application:

1. **How to defend research under pressure.** Judge Q&A sessions prepared him for Caltech's interview process.
2. **Scientific humility.** "My first experiment failed completely. Learning to redesign instead of give up was the most important skill I built."
3. **A network.** A judge who worked at USC introduced him to a summer research program that became a cornerstone of his application.

## Advice for Current Students

> "Pick a question you actually care about. The judges can tell when you are going through the motions. Genuine curiosity is visible — and it is your biggest competitive advantage."

Marcus will begin his undergraduate studies in biology this fall. He has already volunteered to be a mentor in the 2024 Mentor Match Program.
