Last year's grand prize winner, Sarah Chen, took her low-cost water filtration project from the LA Fair to an international symposium in Geneva. We caught up with her to learn what's happened since.

![Sarah Chen](news/posts/2024-02/sarah-chen-spotlight/sarah-chen.jpg)

## From the Fair to the World

Sarah's project, *"A Modular Ceramic Filtration System for Resource-Limited Settings,"* originated as a science class assignment. After winning the LA Fair, she presented at:

- The Intel International Science and Engineering Fair (ISEF) — second place
- The Stockholm Junior Water Prize — finalist
- A WHO water sanitation working group — invited speaker

## Lessons for Current Students

> "Don't try to invent something flashy. Find a real problem and stay close to the people it affects. Your project will be honest, and judges can tell."

Sarah is now a freshman at Caltech studying materials science.
